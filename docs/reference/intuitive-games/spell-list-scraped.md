## Abjuration

### Dispel Magic
- **Description:** As a Reaction when another character uses Divine Blessing, Aura Mastery, casts a spell, or uses a similar ability within 30 feet of your character, you may make a Spellcraft check to counter their ability. Unless your check exceeds the result of the roll for their original check, their action continues without interruption. If your result is greater than theirs, they lose their action with no effect.
- **Advanced:** This spell can be used on existing spells as a two action activity, not just as a reaction.
- **Expert:** You gain advantage on all Spellcraft rolls made with this spell.

### Protection From Elements
- **Description:** Grant elemental resistance against one chosen element equal to your intelligence modifier for yourself or another creature. This effect lasts for a number of minutes equal to your spellcraft modifier. Range: Any touched creature. Special: If you have the Aura Mastery feat, you may affect the same number of creatures with this ability that you can with Aura Mastery and may affect them at the same range.
- **Advanced:** The resistance applies to any type of elemental damage.
- **Expert:** The resistance applies to any type of damage.

### Shield Ally
- **Description:** As a reaction, you may grant an adjacent ally a +2 bonus on any saving throw as soon as the saving throw is called for. The bonus to that saving throw lasts for one round. Range: Any ally within 30 feet.
- **Advanced:** The bonus granted applies to all saving throws, not just reflex saves.
- **Expert:** The spells effects last for one minute.

It lasts for a number of rounds equal to your Intelligence modifier.You know how to make one wall for every two levels that you have acquired. This spell has a range of a number of hexes equal to your Spellcraft modifier.
- **Advanced:** As an action, you can rearrange the wall. You cannot exceed the spell's range and one end point of the wall must remain in place.
- **Expert:** Each section of wall is twice as tall as normal.

- **Stone:** Stone walls have the characteristics of a normal stone wall.
- **Verdant:** Verdant walls have thorns that deal piercing damage to any creature in their square. The damage is equal to your Intelligence modifier, and any creature that takes damage also takes 1d6 points of bleed damage. A character that attempts to move through a verdant wall becomes entangled. The creature may remove this condition by making a step in any direction outside of the wall.

### Portal
- **Description:** As an action, you may relocate yourself to another hex within sight. This hex can be no farther than 5 feet per level from your original location. You can only use this ability once per round.
- **Advanced:** When casting the spell, you can move up to 10 feet per level.
- **Expert:** You do not need to be able to see your destination to teleport there. If the destination is occupied by a creature or object, you must make a DC 20 reflex save or else fall prone in the nearest adjacent square.

### Summon Material
- **Description:** Summon one of the materials listed below. It covers a number of contiguous hexes equal to your level + your intelligence modifier. The materials dissipate after a number of rounds equal to your Spellcraft bonus.
- **Advanced:** You cover twice as much area with this ability.
- **Expert:** As an action, you can rearrange the material. You cannot exceed the spell's range and one end point of the material must remain in place.
- **Fine Particles:** The area becomes difficult terrain as a thick layer of sand or a similar substance appears.
- **Fluids (Water):** Fluids seep up from the ground, carrying a putrid stench. Creatures in the area must make a Fortitude saving throw opposed by your Spellcraft check or else become Sickened. Creatures remain Sickened as long as they remain in the area, and any creature entering the area must make a save.In addition, the area becomes marshy and creatures within the area suffer double damage cold and electricity effects.
- **Gems:** The gems fall from above, and are treated as an attack with which you are proficient, which deals 2d4 piercing damage to all creatures in the area. The gems remain until the spell ends, and all creatures in the area are considered to be flat-footed with their uneven surfaces covering the ground.
- **Metal:** The metal falls from above, and is treated as an attack with which you are proficient, which deals 2d4 slashing damage to all creatures in the area. The metal remains until the spell ends, and damage is dealt again if any creature falls prone in the area. The metal conducts electricity and heat as described in the effects for those elements.
- **Stone:** The rocks fall from above, and are treated as an attack with which you are proficient, which deals 3d6 bludgeoning damage to all creatures in the area. The rocks remain until the spell ends, but settle on the ground leaving normal terrain.
- **Oozes:** Creatures in the area must make a reflex save (opposed by a single Spellcraft check rolled once per round) or else they fall prone.
- **Organic Matter:** Creatures in the area must make a reflex save (opposed by a single Spellcraft check rolled once per round) or else they are entangled.
