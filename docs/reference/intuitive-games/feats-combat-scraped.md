## Combat Feats

Armor Proficiency
- Description You become proficient with all types of armor, and no longer take a penalty on reflex saves based on the DR granted by that armor worn.

Bodyguard
- Description You may spend a reaction giving an adjacent ally a bonus on a reflex save equal to your dexterity modifier. This ability can be activated when an adjacent ally is attacked.

Bowstaff
- Description Requires proficiency with a ranged piercing weapon like a bow. Your bow, crossbow, or firearm can also be used as a melee bludgeoning weapon with which you are proficient. You must spend an action to adjust your grip to use it as melee or ranged. The damage is calculated as if it were a one-handed bludgeoning weapon.

Bludgeoner
- Description When wielding a melee bludgeoning weapon with which you are proficient, you may deal lethal or nonlethal damage with your attacks. You must declare what type of damage you are dealing before an attack is made. When wielding a melee bludgeoning weapon with which you are proficient, you may make a sunder attempt as a free action against an accessible item in possession of a target that you hit with an attack. (Eg: The armor or shield worn by the target). This can only be used once per turn. This sunder attempt does not provoke reactions.

Careful Caster
- Description You do not provoke reactions for casting a spell or using a similar ability with foes threatening you.

Careful Shot
- Description You do not provoke reactions for making a ranged attack with foes threatening you.

Cleave
- Description Requires Dexterity 14 or higher. As a 3-action activity, you may attack all adjacent enemies. You instead attack all threatened enemies with a reach weapon. You don't take the MSP when using this feat.

Combat Patrol
- Description Spend 2 actions activating this feat. Upon activation, you may act as though you took a move action. You may take two extra reactions this turn.

Combat Reflexes
- Description Gain an extra reaction.

Critical Focus
- Description Requires Proficiency with the weapon type chosen. Choose one type of weapon. Eg: One-Handed piercing weapons, Two-Handed firearms, unarmed combat. When wielding weapons from the chosen group with the selected damage type, critical successes with that weapon can be scored with a total of 15 higher than the opponent instead of 20 higher.

Defensive Fighting
- Description Spend one action to use this feat. That action may also be used to make a strike. Take a penalty on all attack rolls equal to your level for one round. Gain a bonus on all reflex rolls equal to your level for one round.

Dodge
- Description When attacked, spend a reaction to multiply your dexterity modifier by 2 on the reflex save against that attack.

Doubleshot
- Description Requires proficiency with ranged weapons. When making an attack with a non-firearm ranged weapon, you may launch an additional piece of ammunition with your attack. Resolve this as one attack that deals damage twice. All subsequent attacks this turn take the MSP penalty as if you had attacked twice with this ability. You may only use this ability once per round.

Duelist
- Description Requires proficiency with one-handed piercing weapons. When wielding a one-handed piercing melee weapon in one hand and not using your other hand for any activities, including holding weapons or a shield, you gain advantage on one attack roll or reflex save that round.

Elemental Mastery
- Description Requires an ability that deals elemental damage. Choose one of the five types of elemental damage. Any time that you use an effect that deals damage of the chosen type, you deal an additional two points of damage. Whenever you take damage from the chosen element, reduce the damage taken by two.

Focused Strike
- Description Requires Int or Wis 14 By spending an additional action when making an attack, you may gain advantage on the attack roll. This ability cannot be applied to attacks made with a reaction or when a character has no actions left to use.

Formidable Arms
- Description Requires proficiency with the weapon group chosen. When wielding a weapon of a chosen group, it deals damage as if it were two size categories larger. Damage dice increase as follows: 1d2, 1d4, 1d6, 1d8, 1d10, 1d12, 1d12+1d4, 1d12+1d6, 1d12+1d8, etc. Weapons that deal 2d6 damage scale as follows: 2d6+1d4, 2d6+1d6, etc.

Guarded Combatant
- Description Requires proficiency with shields. As a two-action activity, you may guard yourself with your shield to make a step or stride and strike. You do not provoke reactions from this movement. If you use the Defensive Fighting feat, the attack made with the strike from this feat does not take the usual penalty on attack rolls from Defensive Fighting.

Great Weapon Fighting
- Description When wielding a melee two-handed weapon or heavy weapon with which you are proficient, you may roll your damage dice two times and use the higher result. When wielding a melee two-handed or heavy weapon with which you are proficient, you may make a reposition attempt as a free action after hitting a target with an attack. This can only be used once per turn. The reposition attempt does not provoke reactions.

Lunge
- Description Spend an action to use this feat. Its effects last until the start of your next turn. The action spent may be used to make an attack while activating this feat. The threatened area with any melee weapons you wield increases by 5 feet. You have disadvantage on reflex saves until the start of your next turn upon activating this feat. If you have this feat and the Relentless feat, you may use the Relentless feat against any creature within 10 feet of you, instead of only adjacent creatures. This allows you to make the Lunge attack against the target provoking the Relentless feat.

Mage Hunter
- Description When you spend a reaction to attack a target that provoked a reaction by casting a spell, using redistribution, or using a similar ability, you gain one extra reaction this turn. Characters with the Careful Caster feat still provoke reactions from you as if they didn’t have the feat.

Magic Specialization
- Description Requires training in Arcane, Nature, or Religion. Your magical prowess has increased, allowing you to enhance your spellcasting in one of the ways listed below. Apply the benefit below to a single Wizard spell, Conduit Power, a connection from the Divine Blessing feat, or a similar ability. Any effect that deals damage deals two additional points of damage of the same type.

Mounted Archery
- Description Requires training in Ride The character is no longer at disadvantage when making ranged attacks while moving on a mount. If you are mounted, you do not provoke reactions for making ranged attacks unless the threatening creature has reach.

Mounted Combat
- Description Requires training in Ride.If a character's mount is about to make a saving throw, the character can spend a reaction to make a Ride check. The mount can use the total from the Ride check or the total from their saving throw.

Mythic Stance: Dragon Stance
- Description Requires no other Mythic Stances. This stance follows all normal stance rules and can be activated using an action. Your first successful attack each turn deals an additional 1d10 points of damage.

Mythic Stance: Fey Stance
- Description Requires no other Mythic Stances. This stance follows all normal stance rules and can be activated using an action. Your movement speed increases by 10 feet.

Mythic Stance: Genie Stance
- Description Requires no other Mythic Stances. This stance follows all normal stance rules and can be activated using an action. When you deal elemental damage, you deal an additional 1d6 points of damage.

Mythic Stance: Griffon Stance
- Description Requires no other Mythic Stances. This stance follows all normal stance rules and can be activated using an action. You gain one extra reaction each turn.

Mythic Stance: Phoenix Stance
- Description Requires no other Mythic Stances. This stance follows all normal stance rules and can be activated using an action. You do not take nonlethal damage from combat or other normal exertion. While this stance is active, you take 1 point of nonlethal damage every 10 minutes.

Mythic Stance: Treant Stance
- Description Requires no other Mythic Stances.This stance follows all normal stance rules and can be activated using an action.If you do not move on your turn, you gain a 1d8 bonus on each attack roll that you make.

Mythic Stance: Unicorn Stance
- Description Requires no other Mythic Stances.This stance follows all normal stance rules and can be activated using an action.You gain a 1d10 bonus on saves against spells, redistribution, Divine Blessing, and similar abilities.

Mythic Stance: Wyvern Stance
- Description Requires no other Mythic Stances.This stance follows all normal stance rules and can be activated using an action.Whenever you deal bleed damage, you double the amount of bleed damage that you deal.

Natural Weapon Mastery
- Description This feat grants one of two potential benefits based on the natural weapons you possess. You may take this feat a second time to gain the advanced feature from your fighting style.
- Single Natural Weapon You multiply your strength modifier by 1.5 instead of the standard strength modifier for your natural weapon. Advanced You roll your damage dice twice whenever you roll damage with this natural weapon, and the damage die increases by one step.
- Multiple Natural Weapons The MSP is lowered to -1 per attack instead of -2 per attack when using multiple of your natural weapons. Advanced If you have made three attacks using your natural weapons on your turn, you make an additional attack with any of your natural weapons. Account for the MSP as normal.

Outflank
- Description When you flank a creature, that creature takes a -2 on all reflex saves while being flanked by you. These penalties stack for each creature who has this feat when those creatures are all flanking the same target.If you have this feat and the Threatening Presence feat, you are considered to be flanking your target if there is a wall or a barrier on the opposite side of your target.

Parry
- Description Make an opposed attack roll as a reaction instead of making a reflex save against a melee attack. If you have access to the Counterattack reaction and the Combat Reflexes feat, you may use one reaction to use Parry and Counterattack against the same attack. Roll two separate attack rolls and resolve the Parry before the Counterattack.

Physical Training
- Description Gain an ability score increase to one physical stat. Gain proficiency with one weapon group.

Polearm Mastery
- Description When wielding a reach weapon with which you are proficient, you may adjust your grip as an action. You have 2 additional ways to wield the weapon as shown below. It takes another action to revert back to the normal grip. You must be using the reach weapon with both hands to use this feat. Double weapon The weapon loses reach and gains double as a property. It deals 1d6 bludgeoning damage on the "off" end Close weapon The weapon loses the reach property and can be wielded as a regular melee weapon. When using this grip, you gain a +2 bonus on attack rolls against creatures that are mounted or are otherwise on a higher level than you.

Point Blank Shot
- Description You gain a +2 bonus on ranged attacks within 30 feet.

Power Attack
- Description You activate this ability using one action, which can be used to make a single attack simultaneously. Upon activation, all melee attacks deal extra damage equal to your level for one round. You take half of your level as a penalty on all melee attacks rounded down. If you round down because your character is an odd-numbered level, achieving partial success on one of your attacks is treated as a failure.

Quickdraw
- Description Draw a weapon as a free action. Any feats that require a move action to adjust your grip are now treated as a free action. Eg: Stock striker, Polearm master, etc.

Quick Shot
- Description Requires proficiency with ranged weapons. If you have spent two actions making two attacks with a ranged weapon, you may make a third attack as a free action. The MSP applies as normal for all attacks.

Reflective Shield
- Description Requires proficiency with shields. As a reaction after being targeted with a ranged attack, you may make an opposed attack roll with your shield to send the attack in another direction instead of making a reflex save. If you succeed, the attack is sent 10 feet in any direction. You may choose to direct it at a particular target within range, in which case your original attack roll must be countered with the target’s reflex save.

Relentless
- Description Use a reaction to both step and strike. The strike may be a trained combat skill.This ability can be activated any time a character steps or strides in a hex adjacent to you, assuming that you have a reaction available to use. If you have this feat and the Lunge feat, you may use the Relentless feat against any creature within the reach of yo r Lunge.

Shield Proficiency
- Description You no longer take penalties when using shields and you can spend an action to ready a shield. This grants an additional +2 bonus on Reflex saves for one round. You can use a shield to gain a bonus on reflex saves and as a weapon at the same time. Normal: You take a penalty on attack rolls equal to the reflex bonus granted by the shield if you are not proficient with the shield you are wielding.

Smite Foe
- Description Requires training in arcane.As an action, you target one creature within sight. You deal additional force damage equal to your Charisma modifier against the target for each attack you make while using this ability. This effect lasts until the beginning of your next turn.

Stock Striker
- Description Requires Proficiency in at least one kind of firearm.Your firearm can also be used as a melee bludgeoning weapon with which you are proficient. You must spend an action to adjust your grip to use it as melee or ranged. The damage is equal to a one or two-handed weapon, based on what type of firearm you are wielding.

Style: Ancient Combat
- Description Requires training in Lore. Upon taking this feat, you gain a move from the list below. You may take this feat an additional time to gain another move from your style. These abilities can only be used when the character is using them with a melee weapon with which they are proficient.
- Seamless Retribution You gain the Counterattack reaction. If you are subject to an attack, you may spend your reaction to make an immediate attack against the aggressor if they are within range of a weapon you are wielding.
- Fast Maneuvers You gain the Redirect reaction. If you avoid an attack with a reflex save, spend a reaction to direct that attack against another character within range of the original attack. This attack uses the attack bonus of whichever weapon you are wielding, with all relevant modifiers, but the damage from the original attack is unchanged.
- Death Spiral As a three-action activity, you attack all enemies you threaten with your melee weapon. You do not take the MSP when using this feat.

Style: Arcane Combat
- Description Requires training in Arcane. Upon taking this feat, you gain a move from the list below. You may take this feat an additional time to gain another move from your style. These abilities can only be used when the character is using them with a melee weapon with which they are proficient.
- Cursed Wound As an action, you may make an attack with your weapon. If the target takes damage from the attack, they are shaken for a number of rounds equal to your Charisma modifier.
- Incorporeal Nudge As a two-action activity, you may make an Arcane check to bring any enemy who is adjacent to a hex where they would normally be within the weapon’s reach into the path of your weapon. They oppose this attack with a reflex save. Treat this check as an attack and deal damage with your weapon as normal if successful. The enemy returns to their original location after the attack is concluded.
- Spell Slash As a reaction when an opponent within reach begins casting a spell, you may make an attack roll opposed by their Reflex Save. If you succeed, you disrupt their spell and they lose the actions that they spent attempting to cast the spell.

Style: Spell Combat
- Description Requires training in Spellcraft. Upon taking this feat, you gain a move from the list below. You may take this feat an additional time to gain another move from your style. These abilities can only be used when the character is using them with a melee weapon with which they are proficient.
- Enchanted Strike As a two-action activity you may make a single attack with a +2 bonus. If this attack hits, you deal additional damage equal to your intelligence modifier. The MSP applies to all attack rolls this round as normal.
- Targeted Strike As a single action activity, you may make a Disarm or Sunder attempt with advantage. The MSP applies to all attack rolls this round as normal.
- True Strike As a three-action activity, you may make an attack with double advantage. The MSP applies to all attack rolls this round as normal.

Style: Wild Combat
- Description Requires training in Nature. Upon taking this feat, you gain a move from the list below. You may take this feat an additional time to gain another move from your style. These abilities can only be used when the character is using them with a melee weapon with which they are proficient.
- Hawk Swoop If you successfully make an acrobatics check to avoid provoking a reaction based on movement, you may make an attack as a free action against the target whose reaction you evaded.
- Stinger Swarm As a single action activity, you may make two attacks against a single target. These attacks always deal -2 damage and the MSP applies as normal.
- Panther’s Leap As a single action activity, you may make a stride or a step and a strike. You may only do this once per turn and may not take any stride or step actions outside of this.

Style: Zealous Combat
- Description Requires training in Religion. Upon taking this feat, you gain a move from the list below. You may take this feat an additional time to gain another move from your style. These abilities can only be used when the character is using them with a melee weapon with which they are proficient.
- Delayed Opportunity You may choose to forgo any number of actions on your turn to gain that many reactions before the beginning of your next turn.
- Obstruction As an action, you make a Religion check opposed by an adjacent target's Will save. If you succeed, the target is at disadvantage on their next attack. This ability does not provoke reactions. You can use this multiple times on the same creature. For each use, they are at disadvantage on another attack.
- Patient Strike When using your reaction to make an attack, you gain advantage on the attack. This does apply when using abilities such as Counterattack or Redirect. It does not apply to effects that use an attack roll like the Parry feat.

Sword and Pistol
- Description Requires Proficiency with One-Handed Firearms and One-Handed Slashing or Piercing weapons.You do not take off-hand penalties when using a One-Handed Slashing or Piercing weapon in your dominant hand and a One-Handed Firearm in your non-dominant hand. You may make attacks against adjacent targets with a OHF without provoking reactions. Your Multiple Strike Penalty is lowered to -1 whenever you use both weapons in the same turn.
- Advanced You may take this feat a second time to gain an additional attack with your off-hand weapon in any round where you make two attacks with your primary weapon and one with your off-hand weapon. The weapons must match the ones described in this feat.

Threatening Presence
- Description You are always considered to be threatening adjacent targets, even when you are not wielding a melee weapon. If you have this feat and the Outflank feat, you are considered to be flanking your target if there is a wall or a barrier on the opposite side of your target.

Throw Anything
- Description Use any item you can easily lift as a thrown weapon without penalty. This counts as an improvised weapon, dealing damage as appropriate for its size.

Two-Weapon Fighting
- Description When attacking with multiple weapons, the MSP is reduced to -1 instead of -2.You now apply your full strength modifier on attack and damage rolls with off-hand weapons. Firearms do not normally apply strength to the damage roll and this feat does not change that.
- Advanced You may take this feat a second time to make one additional attack in any round where you make attacks with weapons wielded in both of your hands.

Weapon Focus
- Description Requires Proficiency with the weapon group chosen. Choose one weapon group. Eg: One-Handed piercing weapons, Two-Handed firearms, unarmed combat. You gain +2 on attack rolls when using weapons from that group with that damage type.
- Advanced You may take this feat multiple times. Each time, it applies to a new weapon group.

Weapon Training
- Description Gain proficiency with any two weapon groups.
- Advanced This feat can be taken multiple times. Each time, it grants proficiency in two additional weapon groups.

Weapon Specialization
- Description Choose one weapon group. Eg: One-Handed piercing weapons, Two-Handed firearms, unarmed combat. You gain +2 on attack rolls when using weapons from that group with that damage type.
- Advanced This feat can be taken multiple times. Each time, it applies to new weapon group.

### Combat Skill Feats

Dirty Trick Mastery
- Description You apply two penalties to any target that you succeed a dirty trick attempt against.

Disarm Mastery
- Description Disarmed opponents are flat footed for one round.

Feint Mastery
- Description If you succeed your feint attempt, the target becomes flat footed until the end of your next turn.

Grapple Mastery
- Description At the beginning of your turn after you have succeeded a grapple attempt, you may maintain the grapple using a free action. A skill check is still required.

Overrun Mastery
- Description You can overrun any number of targets in your path. You make a single overrun attempt and apply it to each target in succession.

Reposition Mastery
- Description When you successfully reposition an opponent, that creature provokes a reaction from all threatening creatures except for you.

Steal Mastery
- Description As a two-action activity, you may stride and make a steal attempt at any point during your movement. This movement does not provoke a reaction from the target of your steal attempt.

Sunder Mastery
- Description You ignore an amount of hardness equal to your level when sundering an object. You may use your sunder skill bonus instead of your attack bonus when attacking a creature that has damage reduction from armor, a thick hide, or some other material.

Trip Mastery
- Description You may make a trip attempt against any target within your reach while making a stride or step action. This counts towards and is affected by the MSP.

Ultramagic
- Description Requires training in Arcane, Nature, or Religion. Your magical prowess has increased, allowing you to enhance your spellcasting as listed below. Apply the benefit to a single Wizard spell, Conduit Power, a connection from the Divine Blessing feat, or a similar ability. You gain advantage on rolls with an effect that requires you to make an arcane, religion, or nature check in order to succeed. If applied to the Redistribution ability, this only affects checks with a single material known.
- Advanced You may take this feat multiple times. Each time, it applies to a new effect.

## Special Feats

Any of the feats below may be selected as either a General Feat or a Combat Feat.

Armor Proficiency
- Description No longer take penalties when wearing armor. Normal: You take a penalty on your reflex saves equal to the DR granted by the armor when not proficient.

Boundless Stamina
- Description You gain a +2 on Fortitude saves.

Daring Quickness
- Description You gain a +2 on Reflex saves.

Inspiring Insight
- Description You gain a +2 on Will saves.

Shield Proficiency
- Description You no longer take penalties when using shields and you can spend an action to ready a shield. This grants an additional +2 bonus on Reflex saves for one round. You can use a shield to gain a bonus on reflex saves and as a weapon at the same time. Normal: You take a penalty on attack rolls equal to the reflex bonus granted if you are not proficient with the shield you are wielding.

Prodigy
- Description Gain proficiency in a new skill.
- Advanced You may select this feat multiple times. Each time you select it, you gain proficiency in a new skill.

Versatile
- Description Acquire another trait. As with most feats, you can only select this feat one time.

Weapon Training
- Description Gain proficiency with two weapon groups.
- Advanced This feat can be taken multiple times. Each time, it grants proficiency in two additional weapon groups.

### Ability Score Feats

Strength
- Description Gain an Ability Score Boost to strength. Reroll one strength-based skill check once per day.

Dexterity
- Description Gain an Ability Score Boost to dexterity. Reroll one dexterity-based skill check once per day.

Constitution
- Description Gain an Ability Score Boost to constitution. Instead of falling unconscious at 0 HP or less, you remain conscious for one additional turn unless you are killed.

Intelligence
- Description Gain an Ability Score Boost to intelligence. Reroll one intelligence-based skill check once per day.

Wisdom
- Description Gain an Ability Score Boost to wisdom. Reroll one wisdom-based skill check once per day.

Charisma
- Description Gain an Ability Score Boost to charisma. Reroll one charisma-based skill check once per day.
