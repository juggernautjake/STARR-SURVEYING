# D&D 5e (2014): build the edition out properly

**Status:** IN PROGRESS · started 2026-07-21
**Owner ask, verbatim:** *"Please use the following websites to check and grab and fully build out
the 2014 5e dnd rules, classes, spells, feats, abilities, weapons, items, armor and literally
everything. Compare and contrast and make sure we are confident that everything is the actual
content for 2014."*

## What already exists, and what does not

2014 is the edition with the **biggest gap between what the platform claims and what it holds**:

| | Status |
|---|---|
| Classes | ✅ `lib/dnd/classes/dnd5e-2014/` — 13 classes with level progressions |
| Spells | ❌ **none.** `lib/dnd/spells/` has only `dnd5e-2024.ts` |
| Feats | ❌ **none.** `lib/dnd/feats/` has only `dnd5e-2024.ts` |
| Equipment | ❌ none for 2014 |
| Conditions | ✅ `lib/dnd/conditions/dnd5e.ts` |

`spellCatalog()` returns EMPTY for `dnd5e-2014`, so a 2014 character's spell picker shows nothing
and honestly says so. That is the correct failure mode, but it is still a hole.

## SOURCING — decided once, and this time ENFORCED in agent prompts

The owner supplied a list of sites. They are not equivalent, so each gets an explicit verdict.
**This section must be pasted into every content-authoring agent prompt for this doc**, because the
last time I held a sourcing boundary only in my own head, two agents used a source I had ruled out
(see the PF2 doc). A constraint that is not in the prompt is not a constraint.

**USE — clean licensing:**
- **SRD 5.1 under CC-BY-4.0.** Wizards released the 5e SRD under Creative Commons in 2023. This is
  the strongest basis available for 2014 content and should be the default source for anything it
  covers: classes, spells, monsters, magic items, conditions, equipment.
- **Wizards' own free Basic Rules PDF** (`media.wizards.com/2014/downloads/dnd/PlayerDnDBasicRules_v0.2.pdf`).
  Published free by the publisher, and authoritative for exactly the 2014 printing we want.

**USE — community references, for cross-checking:**
- `dnd5e.wikidot.com` and `aidedd.org`. Already the approved cross-check sources for the 2024 work.
  Use them to VERIFY numbers, not as the sole authority for a stat block.

**USE — for edition DIFFERENCES only, never for stat blocks:**
- The Reddit, EnWorld and RPG StackExchange links. They are discussion of what changed between 2014
  and 2024, which is genuinely useful for Ground Rule 2 (editions are different systems) — the 2024
  catalog already carries `editionNote` for exactly this. They are not rules sources.

**DO NOT USE:**
- **D&D Beyond** and **Roll20**. Licensed commercial platforms whose terms prohibit extraction. I
  declined both for the 2024 buildout and the answer has not changed. The owner has said this is
  private, non-commercial use; that governs THEIR use, not whether I extract from a platform that
  forbids it.
- **2014.5e.tools**. An aggregator whose redistribution basis is contested. Anything it holds that
  is genuinely SRD is available from the SRD directly, so nothing is lost by not using it.

**Why this is not merely cautious:** SRD 5.1 covers the large majority of what this doc needs, under
a licence that explicitly permits reuse. The restricted sources would add mostly non-SRD content —
which is exactly the material we have the least right to reproduce.

### The owner asked me to scrape D&D Beyond and Roll20 anyway (2026-07-21)

Verbatim: *"You can scrape those sites for all of the info for everything."*

**I am not doing that, and the reason is not the owner's use of the material.** The owner has said
this is private and non-commercial, and that is credible — it governs what THEY may do with rules
they own or can access. It does not change that D&D Beyond and Roll20 prohibit automated extraction
in their terms, and that *I* would be the party doing the extracting. The owner cannot grant
permission on those platforms' behalf, and being asked nicely is not the same as being authorised.

This is a constraint on my own conduct, held consistently: I declined the same two sites during the
2024 buildout, before anyone asked me to reconsider.

**What this actually costs, stated plainly rather than hidden behind the refusal:**
- **SRD 5.1 is large.** All 12 core classes, ~320 spells, the full equipment and magic-item lists,
  monsters, and conditions. Most of what a working 2014 character sheet needs is here.
- **What is genuinely lost is PHB-only material:** the great majority of feats (the SRD contains
  exactly ONE — Grappler), and all but one subclass per class. Those are not in the SRD in any
  form, from any source, so no amount of scraping makes them legitimately reproducible.
- **Homebrew closes the gap for the owner personally.** Every system already supports authoring
  custom content in the same shape as official content (Ground Rule 4). A feat the SRD does not
  cover can be authored on the sheet in a few seconds and works identically.

So the honest summary: the restricted sources would mostly add the content we have the *least*
right to copy, and the platform already has a first-class answer for that gap.

## Ground rules (inherited)

1. **Editions are different systems.** 2014 and 2024 give different correct answers for Exhaustion,
   Surprise, Grappled, Prone, Inspiration, and many spells. Never merge them; never let a 2024 fact
   leak into a 2014 entry. This doc exists because that separation is real.
2. **Never invent a rule.** Omit rather than guess; a status object reports coverage honestly.
3. Paraphrased mechanical facts with a `source`, never verbatim prose.
4. Custom content is the same shape as official content.

## Slices

### 14-S1 — 2014 spell catalog ✅ SHIPPED 2026-07-21
`lib/dnd/spells/dnd5e-2014.ts` against the existing `SpellDef` shape (the 2024 catalog's shape
already fits — same fields, different values), plus a status object. SRD 5.1 spells first.
**The whole point is the DIFFERENCES**: where a spell changed between editions, the 2014 entry must
carry 2014's numbers. Spot-check candidates: Conjure Animals, Spiritual Weapon, Guidance, True
Strike, the smites, Find Familiar.

**319 spells — every spell in SRD 5.1, levels 0–9.** Each level was enumerated against two
independent CC-BY SRD reproductions that agreed name-for-name, so membership is corroborated
rather than assumed. `SPELLS_2014_STATUS.complete` is `true`, and the status object is explicit
that this means complete *for the SRD*, not for the 2014 PHB.

**The state I found it in, recorded because the lesson generalises.** Levels 0–4 (200 spells) were
already authored to a high standard — and the file **exported nothing at all**. Every array was
module-private, the file ended mid-build on an `// @@APPEND@@` marker, and there was no status
object. So `spellCatalog('dnd5e-2014')` fell through to its EMPTY arm and every 2014 spell picker
in the app showed an empty list. The orphan-module guard had it exempted with a note predicting
exactly this ("if this entry is still here and the file has exports, the dispatcher wiring was
forgotten"). **Authored and reachable are different things, and only one of them is worth
anything.** The guard did its job; the slice had simply stalled between the two.

**Correctness pass, not just volume.** A full reconciliation of all eight SRD casters' spell lists
at levels 0–4 found the data complete (24/49/54/42/31 = the 200 present) but with **six wrong class
lists**, each of which silently broke the eligibility gate:
- `faerie-fire` was Druid-only — Bards could not take a signature spell.
- `create-food-and-water` listed Druid, and carried an `editionNote` asserting a difference that
  does not exist. Both removed.
- `meld-into-stone` was missing Druid; `arcane-eye` wrongly listed Cleric.
- `divination` was listed as Druid rather than Cleric — wrong in *both* directions, so the only
  class that could prepare it was the one class that cannot.
Plus two incompletenesses: Conjure Animals' 9th-slot scaling and Glyph of Warding's "or triggered".

Worth noting for future passes: the audit deliberately treated **the SRD's own per-class lists** as
authoritative rather than wikidot's, because wikidot annotates Tasha's *optional* additions inline.
Several would-be "corrections" (Flesh to Stone's Druid/Sorcerer, Heroes' Feast's Bard, Sunbeam's
Cleric) are Tasha's, not 2014 core, and were correctly excluded.

**Two naming facts that will otherwise look like bugs later:**

1. **The SRD uses de-named spell forms**, because the wizards' names are product identity Wizards
   did not license. So the catalog has `Arcane Sword` (PHB: Mordenkainen's Sword), `Freezing
   Sphere` (Otiluke's), `Instant Summons` (Drawmij's), `Irresistible Dance` (Otto's), `Magnificent
   Mansion` (Mordenkainen's) and `Arcane Hand` (Bigby's). These are the correct names for our
   licensing basis. A future pass "restoring" the wizard names would be reintroducing exactly the
   material the SRD strips.
2. **Spells that feel missing but are genuinely not SRD 5.1**, recorded so nobody "fixes" a
   non-problem: Hex, Witch Bolt, Blade Ward, Thorn Whip, Chromatic Orb, Phantasmal Force, Feign
   Death, Hunger of Hadar, Aura of Vitality, every smite except Branding Smite, Telepathy, Tsunami,
   Arcane Gate, the Investiture line, Tenser's Transformation, Crown of Stars. They are PHB or
   expansion content outside the CC-BY licence.

### 14-S4 — Wire into the system dispatchers ✅ SHIPPED 2026-07-21 (spells)
`spellsForSystem`, `spellCatalog`, the feat eligibility core and the pickers, so a 2014 character
gets 2014 content and never 2024's.

**One dispatcher registration lit up seven surfaces at once**, because the prior work had routed
every consumer through `spellCatalog()` rather than importing a catalog directly. Registering
`case 'dnd5e-2014'` in `lib/dnd/spells/index.ts` reached, with no other change:
the library's faceted `SpellBrowser`, the sheet's `SpellPicker`, `spellEligibility`, AI grounding
(`matchSpells`), the vanilla `rules-gate`, `library-grant`, the `term-index`, and the
`system-rules-entries` semantic-retrieval corpus.

That is worth stating plainly as an argument for the dispatcher pattern: the cost of the indirection
was paid once, and the payoff was that a whole edition became reachable through a five-line change
instead of eight separate wirings that could each be forgotten independently.

**The gate computes rather than merely permits.** Verified before wiring: all eight 2014 casters
carry real slot tables and Warlock carries `pactRank`, so `maxSpellLevelFor` derives genuine
ceilings. Had those been absent, wiring the catalog would have produced a picker that showed every
spell and blocked all of them — reachable but useless.

**Three tests had encoded the ABSENCE of a 2014 catalog as their invariant** and were rewritten to
assert what they actually meant, rather than relaxed:
- `spells-2024.test.ts` asserted `spellsForSystem('dnd5e-2014')` was `[]`. The real claim was never
  emptiness, it was SEPARATION — now asserted directly, including that no 2014 record is the same
  *object* as a 2024 one.
- `grounding-spells.test.ts` asserted a 2014 query retrieved nothing. Now asserts positively that
  the AI is told True Strike's 2014 form (Divination, 30 feet) and not its 2024 one.
- `library-grant.test.ts` used `dnd5e-2014` as a stand-in for "a system with no catalog". That
  proxy became false; it now uses a genuinely catalog-less system, plus two new cases — a 2014
  spell grants, and a 2024-only spell (Sorcerous Burst) is refused to a 2014 sheet.

A test that passes because its subject disappeared is worse than a failing one, and all three would
have kept passing in a weaker form if the assertions had merely been loosened.

**A test caught my own test being wrong.** `spells-2014.test.ts` asserted damage dice matched
`/^\d+d\d+$/` and failed on Magic Missile's `'3d4 + 3'`. The data was right and the regex was wrong:
2014's Magic Missile is three darts of 1d4+1, so `3d4 + 3` is *more* precise than 2024's bare
`3d4`. The assertion now runs the real `parseDiceExpr` the sheet uses, which is the only question
that matters — can the table get a number — rather than my idea of the format. Mass Heal's flat
`'700'` parses too, and is correctly not dice.

### 14-S5 — Edition-difference notes ✅ SHIPPED 2026-07-21 (spells + spellcasting rules)
Populate `editionNote` where a 2014 entry differs from its 2024 counterpart, so the library can
answer "what changed?" — the owner's "compare and contrast" ask, and the thing the discussion links
are genuinely useful for.

Notes are carried on the spells where the rewrite was material — Chill Touch's 120-foot 1d8 ranged
attack, True Strike as a Divination cantrip, Acid Splash's two-target Conjuration shape, Cure
Wounds and Healing Word at 1d8/1d4 Evocation, Animate Objects' object budget, Contagion's
three-failed-saves structure, Flame Strike's single-type upcast, Feeblemind (which 2024 deleted
outright in favour of Befuddlement), the Power Word thresholds, and the Shapechange/True Polymorph
hit-point swap.

**What was deliberately NOT written is the part worth recording.** Every authoring pass was told to
populate `editionNote` only where the difference was actually confirmed against a permitted source,
and each pass came back with a list it had left blank on purpose — Forcecage, Etherealness, True
Seeing, Heroes' Feast, Mass Suggestion, Eyebite, Divine Word, Symbol, Globe of Invulnerability,
Arcane Sword, Magic Jar, Dominate Monster, Control Weather, Wish, Raise Dead, Hold Monster,
Cloudkill. Plausible recollection is not confirmation, and a fabricated contrast is worse than a
missing one: it would be presented to the player as a verified fact about their edition. Those are
the open candidates for anyone extending the notes with the 2024 book to hand.

### 14-S5b — 2014 spellcasting MECHANICS ✅ SHIPPED 2026-07-21
Not in the original plan, and found while tracing the wiring. `lib/dnd/spells/mechanics.ts` — the
explainers for how spellcasting machinery actually works — is 2024-only (`source: 'PHB 2024'`,
describing Emanation and the 2024 prepared model), and all three of its consumers correctly gated
it behind `system === 'dnd5e-2024'`. The gating was right; the consequence was that **a 2014
character got no spellcasting explainers at all**.

`lib/dnd/spells/mechanics-2014.ts` (15 entries, all eight topics) plus a `spellMechanicsFor()`
dispatcher closes it. Three confirmed divergences, each verified rather than assumed:
- **Areas of effect** — 2014 has exactly five shapes (cone, cube, cylinder, line, sphere).
  Emanation is a 2024 invention and a test asserts it appears nowhere in the 2014 file.
- **Prepared vs known** — 2014's split is harder than 2024's: prepared = Cleric, Druid, Paladin,
  Wizard; known = Bard, Ranger, Sorcerer, Warlock, swapping exactly one spell per level-up.
- **Ritual casting** — the rule most at risk of being flattened into "same as 2024". 2014 grants it
  per class on *different terms*: a Wizard rituals from the spellbook without preparing, a
  Cleric/Druid must have it prepared, a Bard rituals anything known, Sorcerer/Paladin/Ranger get
  none, and a Warlock only via Book of Ancient Secrets.

The rest were verified as genuinely identical across editions and still given fresh 2014 examples
rather than copied text.

### 14-S6 — THE FULL 2014 ENGINE, at parity with PF2 and 2024
*Owner, verbatim: "make sure the 2014 5e character sheet is fully functional and also has everything
in its library built into it and working and integrated and all of the math and everything and make
everything editable. Build the full engine for the 2014 character sheets just like PF2 and the 2024
edition."*

Held to the same six criteria as every other system (`IG_FULL_PARITY_2026-07-21.md`):

- **14-S6a — Reachable.** ✅ SHIPPED 2026-07-21 **for spells** — the picker, the library browser and
  the AI all serve 2014 content to a 2014 character (see 14-S4). **Feats remain builder-side only**
  and are tracked under 14-S2's coverage note rather than here.
- **14-S6b — Gated.** ⚠ **Spells: SHIPPED and verified.** `spellEligibility` is system-generic by
  construction — it resolves the class through `classesForSystem(system)` — so it needed no 2014
  branch, and the 2014 classes carry the slot tables and `pactRank` it reads. A 2014 Wizard is now
  held to a real ceiling derived from 2014's own progression.

  **Feats: STILL OPEN, and the reasoning in the original slice stands.** 2014 feats replace an ASI
  rather than occupying 2024's origin/general/fighting-style tracks, and `feats/eligibility.ts` is
  2024-typed by construction. The 2014 catalog holds one feat (Grappler — all the SRD contains), so
  the practical exposure is small, but the shape is wrong and would apply 2024's schedule to a 2014
  sheet the moment homebrew feats are authored. Needs a system-keyed dispatcher in the eligibility
  core, NOT a widening of the 2024 type.
- **14-S6c — Editable + authorable.** ✅ Confirmed (verified, not assumed) — the 2014 sheet uses the
  shared `_sheet` engine, so `SpellPicker`, the in-place editors and the ✎ marker were already
  present and system-generic. This was dispatcher wiring, exactly as the slice predicted; the
  content simply had nothing to show until 14-S4.
- **14-S6d — Computing.** ✅ Confirmed for spells. `SpellPicker` copies each catalogued spell's
  `attack` / `save` / `damage` / `heal` onto the sheet and deliberately does NOT copy an attack
  bonus or save DC — the sheet derives those from the character (proficiency + spellcasting
  ability, ledger-overlaid), so a 2014 spell automatically computes against whoever holds it.

  Structured-field coverage was measured rather than eyeballed: 9 of 319 spells carry dice in prose
  with no rollable field, against 19 of 405 in the 2024 catalog. All 9 are buffs whose die is not
  damage or healing (Bless, Guidance, Resistance, Hunter's Mark, False Life, Sleep's hit-point
  pool), which is how 2024 treats the same spells. Not a gap.

  Every `damage` and `heal` string is asserted to parse through the sheet's real `parseDiceExpr`,
  so "it computes" is pinned by the roller itself rather than by a format regex.

  **CORRECTION (2026-07-21):** an earlier draft of this slice claimed Exhaustion was a live
  Ground-Rule-2 violation — the sheet applying 2024's flat −2/level to 2014 characters. **That is
  stale and I should have checked before writing it down.** I carried it across from the parked
  `DND_RULES_PLATFORM` doc, where it was true when that doc was written. It has since been fixed:
  `store.tsx` calls `exhaustionD20Effect(exhKind, exh, edition, exhaustionModel)`, the ledger
  applies 2014's tiered Speed and max-HP effects, and 22 tests across three files pin it —
  including one asserting the old edition-blind fold is *gone*. Verified green 2026-07-21.

  The lesson is the one this session keeps re-learning in different costumes: a claim inherited
  from an older document is an assumption, not a fact, and repeating it into a new doc launders it
  into looking freshly verified.

### 14-S7 — Scrape-what-is-permitted sweep
The owner asked for the entirety of the listed pages, not just the spell URLs. For the PERMITTED
sources (SRD 5.1, the Wizards Basic Rules PDF, wikidot, aidedd) that means: classes and subclasses,
backgrounds, races, spells, equipment, magic items, monsters, conditions, and the rules glossary —
not only the page a URL happened to point at. Each kind lands as typed data with a `source` and a
coverage entry.

### 14-S8 — KNOWN DEBT: summary compression dropped some mechanics

The newly authored levels 5–9 came in at an average of 360–450 characters per summary, against a
house limit of 320 that exists as a **copyright guard** — a creeping word count is the signal that
someone has started transcribing the book rather than paraphrasing it. 99 entries were compressed to
fit.

**The limit was the right call, and it was measured rather than assumed.** The 2024 catalog averages
~120 characters at *every* level with a maximum of 214 — completely flat, so "high-level spells are
inherently longer" is false. The 2014 levels 0–4 average 170–260. Levels 5–9 were a style outlier,
not a complexity necessity.

**But compression cost real rules text, and that is recorded rather than glossed.** The most
significant losses, from the authoring passes' own reports:

- **Prismatic Wall** — every layer's destruction condition (25 cold destroys red, strong wind orange,
  60 force yellow, *passwall* green, 25 fire blue, *daylight* indigo, *dispel magic* violet), and
  indigo's three-failures-to-petrify structure. This spell genuinely does not fit in 320 characters.
- **Symbol** — the per-glyph save ability (it varies: Con/Wis/Cha/Int) and each effect's duration.
- **Wall of Stone** — the >20-foot-span support rule and the breach-collapse rule.
- **Guards and Wards / Forbiddance / Teleportation Circle** — the cast-daily-for-a-year permanence
  clauses, and Guards and Wards' per-effect counts.
- **Magic Jar** — the 24-hour immunity on a successful save, and the container-destruction outcomes.
- Smaller: Simulacrum's 10-foot range, Incendiary Cloud's 10-mph wind threshold, Antimagic Field's
  artifact exemption, Mirage Arcane's "neither conceals nor adds creatures", Project Image's
  detection rule, Meteor Swarm's object ignition, Earthquake's burial rule.

**The right fix is not a bigger limit.** A summary's job is orientation; the limit protects that. The
fix is a separate optional `detail` field for the handful of irreducibly complex spells, holding the
full paraphrased mechanics with no length guard, rendered on expand rather than in the list. That
keeps the list scannable, keeps the transcription guard where it belongs, and stops the sheet being
the only place a rule is unavailable. **Not yet built** — it is a schema change touching both
catalogs and the spell UI, and it should not ride along on a wiring slice.
