## 1. EXAM FORMAT

| Attribute | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Delivery | Computer-based test (CBT), **closed book** with an **electronic reference** | NCEES FS CBT Specs (eff. July 2020) |
| Questions | **110 questions** | NCEES FS Specs; ncees.org/exams/fs-exam |
| Total appointment | **6 hours** | NCEES FS Specs |
| Time breakdown | Nondisclosure agreement 2 min · Tutorial 8 min · **Exam 5 hr 20 min** · Scheduled break 25 min | ncees.org/exams/fs-exam |
| Units | Both **SI** and **U.S. Customary (USCS)** | NCEES FS Specs |
| Where offered | **Pearson VUE** (NCEES-approved Pearson test centers), administered **year-round** in testing windows | ncees.org/exams/fs-exam |
| Fee | **$225** payable directly to NCEES | ncees.org/exams/fs-exam (subject to change) |
| Scoring | **Pass/fail** (no numeric score released). Results typically in **7–10 business days** via MyNCEES; fails get a diagnostic report by topic | ncees.org/exams/fs-exam |
| Target audience | Recent grads / students near completion of an undergraduate surveying degree (ABET-accredited program) | ncees.org/exams/fs-exam |

### Licensure path (FS is the FIRST step)
```
FS exam (pass)  →  Surveyor-in-Training (SIT) / intern designation
                →  ~4 yrs qualifying experience under a licensed PS
                →  PS exam (Principles & Practice of Surveying)
                →  state-specific exam (in TX: TSSE)
                →  Professional Surveyor (in TX: RPLS) license
```
- The FS is the surveying analog of the FE (engineering). Passing it earns the
  **intern / "in-training"** credential. The **engineering** equivalent is **EIT**;
  for **surveying** the credential is generally **SIT (Surveyor-in-Training)**.
- **"SIT" is the Texas designation** for the surveying intern credential, issued by
  **TBPELS** (Texas Board of Professional Engineers and Land Surveyors) after passing
  the FS. Full TX path: SIT → 4000 hrs (≈2 yrs full-time) responsible-charge time under
  a TX RPLS → pass PS → pass **Texas Specific Surveying Exam (TSSE)** → apply for **RPLS**.
  Source: TBPELS (pels.texas.gov); nlcprep.com "How to Become a Land Surveyor in Texas."

### Recent changes / currency flags
- Current exam spec is **"Effective Beginning with the July 2020 Examinations"** — this
  is the spec still in force as of the 2024 republished PDF. **No newer content spec has
  superseded it** as of access date. ⚠️ Confirm on ncees.org before an exam cycle.
- The exam moved to year-round CBT (Pearson VUE) years ago; legacy pencil-and-paper
  references are obsolete.

## 2. KNOWLEDGE AREAS + QUESTION COUNTS (official spec)

NCEES publishes **question RANGES** per area (not fixed percentages). Ranges overlap
because item placement varies form-to-form; they do **not** sum to exactly 110.
Source: NCEES "Fundamentals of Surveying (FS) CBT Exam Specifications," eff. July 2020
(PDF: ncees.org/wp-content/uploads/FS-CBT-specs.pdf and .../2024/04/FS-CBT-specs-2024.pdf).

| # | Knowledge Area | # Questions (range) | Approx. % of 110 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Surveying Processes and Methods** | **16–24** | ~15–22% |
| 2 | **Mapping Processes and Methods** | **14–21** | ~13–19% |
| 3 | **Boundary Law and Real Property Principles** | **19–29** | ~17–26% |
| 4 | **Surveying Principles** (incl. geodesy) | **13–20** | ~12–18% |
| 5 | **Survey Computations and Computer Applications** | **17–26** | ~15–24% |
| 6 | **Business Concepts** (incl. ethics) | **11–17** | ~10–15% |
| 7 | **Applied Mathematics and Statistics** | **10–15** | ~9–14% |

> ⚠️ The 2024 republished PDF has a garbled number column; the cleaner undated spec PDF
> gives the ranges above and they are internally consistent. Area 3 (Boundary Law) is the
> single largest area — boundary/legal is the heaviest topic on the FS.

### Subtopics per area (verbatim from spec)

**1. Surveying Processes and Methods (16–24)**
A. Instrumentation (GNSS/GPS, levels, total stations, robotic total stations, scanners, UAS)
B. GNSS/GPS surveys (static, kinematic, OPUS, real-time networks)
C. Control surveys (horizontal, vertical, network design, accuracy standards)
D. Cadastral (PLSS, boundary, metes and bounds, land title)
E. Topographic surveys
F. Construction surveys (layout, as-built, quantity)
G. Land development (subdivision design/platting, land use, environmental, flood plains, wetlands)
H. Field record keeping and documentation (procedures, field books, raw data files)

**2. Mapping Processes and Methods (14–21)**
A. Basic mapping concepts (scaling, symbols, features, legend, contours, cartography)
B. Types of maps (plan and profile, cross section, plat, record of survey, ALTA, topographic, planimetric)
C. CAD (2-D, 3-D, BIM)
D. GIS (feature collection, map projections, coordinate systems, metadata, database design, spatial data analysis, applications)
E. Digital terrain model (machine control, TIN, digital surface model, digital elevation model)
F. Photogrammetry and remote sensing (close range, conventional, softcopy, ground control, QC, flight/project planning, UAS, drone, LiDAR, satellite, digital image analysis)

**3. Boundary Law and Real Property Principles (19–29)**
A. Public records and descriptions (land descriptions, mineral rights, ownership rights, weighting evidence)
B. Common law principles (controlling elements, unwritten rights, adverse possession)
C. Easements (granted, implied/prescriptive)
D. Simultaneous and sequential conveyances
E. Metes and bounds
F. PLSS
G. Water law (riparian, littoral rights, water marks/levels)
H. Sources of law (federal/state/local, administrative, common, citations, legal research)
I. Encumbrances (restrictive covenants, mortgages, liens)
J. Real property law (deeds, chains of title)

**4. Surveying Principles (13–20)**
A. Basic surveying (horizontal/vertical surveys, historical methods/instruments, route surveying, magnetic declination)
B. Geodesy (spherical trig, geometric/physical, geodetic coordinates, orthometric corrections, convergence, geodetic reductions, gravity modeling, geoid modeling)
C. Applied geodesy (datums & datum conversions, lat/long, coordinate transformations, **SPCS state plane**, map projections, control networks, reduction of observations, deflection of vertical, satellite coordinate systems)

**5. Survey Computations and Computer Applications (17–26)**
A. Coordinate geometry
B. Traverse closure and adjustments
C. Leveling (differential, trigonometric, reciprocal, precise)
D. Least squares adjustments
E. Area
F. Horizontal curves
G. Vertical curves
H. Volume (mass diagrams, earthwork)
I. Spreadsheets
J. Slopes and grades

**6. Business Concepts (11–17)**
A. Project planning (resource management, scheduling, cost estimation, tracking)
B. Safety (signage, basic first aid, safety equipment)
C. Liabilities (negligence, employee behavior, errors and omissions)
D. Contracts (basic elements, scope of work, specifications)
E. Supervision (team leadership, personnel management)
F. Project documentation and record management
G. **Ethics**
H. Communication (written, oral, alternate forms, conflict resolution)

**7. Applied Mathematics and Statistics (10–15)**
A. College mathematics (trig, analytical geometry & calculus, linear algebra & matrix theory)
B. Probability and statistics (mean, median, mode, hypothesis testing, normal distribution, linear regression)
C. Measurement science (error analysis, error propagation, positional tolerance/accuracy, random/systematic/blunder errors, unit conversions)
D. Quantitative reasoning (critical thinking, data analysis/validation, blunder detection, data quality, redundancy)

## 3. THE FS REFERENCE HANDBOOK

- During the exam, examinees get an **electronic NCEES FS Reference Handbook** plus a set
  of **searchable electronic PDF standards** (one document open at a time). The handbook
  and standards are available the **entire exam**. **No personal references are allowed.**
- Current published version: **FS Reference Handbook 2.4 (released March 2024)**.
  ⚠️ NCEES revs these periodically — verify the exact current version in your MyNCEES
  account before testing; 2.5+ may exist by your exam date.
  Source: NCEES Knowledge Base (help.ncees.org/article/87); prep-source corroboration.
- **Download a free study copy** from your **MyNCEES account** (account.ncees.org).
  NCEES does **not** sell the handbook or standards; the free PDF may be printed for study.
- **Handbook contents (v2.x):** Abbreviations & acronyms · Conversions and useful
  relationships (metric prefixes, unit conversions, constants) · Mathematical & surveying
  formulas — quadratic, trigonometry, triangles, **horizontal/vertical curves**,
  **photogrammetry**, physics, **geodesy**, **EDM**, **area formulas**, **earthwork/volume**,
  **probability & statistics** · Economics · Ethics.
- **Standards supplied on the exam** (scored to the revision year shown):
  - **ALTA/NSPS** Minimum Standard Detail Requirements for Land Title Surveys, **2021**
  - **FEMA** Elevation Certificate and Instructions, **2023**
  - **FGCS** Geodetic Leveling spec §3.5 (digital/bar-code leveling), **2004**
  - **GPAS** Geospatial Positioning Accuracy Standards (Parts 1–3 1998, Part 4 2002, Part 5 2005)
  - **NSPS Model Standards** Sections A–H, **2002**
  - **USNMAS** US National Map Accuracy Standards, **1947**

## 4. CALCULATOR POLICY (NCEES-approved list)

NCEES limits calculators to a fixed approved list, **reviewed annually**. The same list
applies to FS, FE, PS, and PE. **No programmable, graphing, or CAS calculators.**
Source: ncees.org/exams/calculator (2026 exam list).

**Approved models for 2026 exams:**

| Brand | Allowed models |
|---|---|
| **Casio** | **All fx-115** and **fx-991** models (model name must contain "fx-115" or "fx-991") |
| **Hewlett Packard** | **HP 33s** and **HP 35s** only (no other HP) |
| **Texas Instruments** | **All TI-30X** and **TI-36X** models (model name must contain "TI-30X" or "TI-36X") |

**Practical buying advice for learning an approved calculator:**
- **TI-36X Pro** — the most commonly recommended FS/FE calculator; supports degrees-minutes-
  seconds (DMS), polar↔rectangular conversion, matrices, stats, and solver. Best all-rounder.
- **Casio fx-991EX (ClassWiz)** or **fx-115ES Plus** — fast, large display, good DMS & stat.
- **HP 35s** — RPN option; favored by some surveyors for COGO-style chaining, but discontinued.
- ⚠️ HP 33s/35s are **discontinued** — buy a TI-36X Pro or Casio fx-991 instead.
- Confirm the **current year's** list on ncees.org before the exam; the list is re-issued annually.

**Calculator skills to drill (high-value on FS):**
- DMS ↔ decimal degrees, and trig in DMS mode
- Polar ↔ rectangular (bearing/distance ↔ ΔE/ΔN) — core for COGO/inverse
- Matrix entry (for least squares / linear algebra items)
- Statistics mode (mean, std dev, linear regression)
- Memory/store-recall to chain traverse computations without re-keying

## 5. HIGH-YIELD TOPICS & FORMULAS (by area)

### Error theory & statistics (Area 7 + 5D)
- Mean, std deviation; **standard error of the mean** = σ/√n
- **Error propagation**: sum E_sum = √(ΣEᵢ²); series of n equal measurements E_series = E·√n
- **Random vs systematic vs blunder**; 68/95/99.7 normal-curve probabilities
- Weighted means (weight ∝ 1/σ²); relative accuracy / precision ratio (e.g., 1:10,000)

### Leveling (5C)
- Differential leveling: ΣBS − ΣFS = ΔElev; HI = Elev + BS; Elev = HI − FS
- **Curvature & refraction** correction ≈ 0.0206 K² (m, K in km) or 0.0206 (combined factor); reciprocal leveling cancels it
- Trigonometric leveling: ΔElev = S·sin(α) (+ hi − hr), with C&R correction

### Distance / EDM / angle (1A, 5)
- EDM corrections: instrument constant, scale, **temperature/pressure (ppm)** atmospheric correction
- **Slope→horizontal**: H = S·cos(zenith) or H = S·cos(vertical angle)
- Taping corrections: temperature, tension/pull, sag, slope, standardization
- Angle/distance error budgets; angular misclosure = (n known sum) check

### Traverse (5B, 5E)
- **Latitude** = L·cos(bearing/azimuth); **Departure** = L·sin(...)
- **Linear misclosure** = √(ΣLat² + ΣDep²); **relative precision** = misclosure / perimeter
- **Compass (Bowditch) rule** adjustment: correction to lat/dep ∝ side length / perimeter
- **Area by coordinates** (shoelace): A = ½|Σ(Xᵢ(Y_{i+1} − Y_{i−1}))|
- Area by DMD/double meridian distance (legacy but still tested)

### Coordinate geometry / inverse (5A)
- **Inverse**: distance = √(ΔE²+ΔN²); azimuth = atan2(ΔE, ΔN) → fix quadrant
- Forward: E₂ = E₁ + D·sin(Az); N₂ = N₁ + D·cos(Az)
- Intersections: bearing-bearing, bearing-distance, distance-distance

### Azimuth / bearing conversions (1, 5)
- Bearing↔azimuth by quadrant (NE = Az; SE = 180−Az; SW = 180+Az; NW = 360−Az)
- Angle-to-the-right traverse: forward azimuth = back azimuth ± interior angle
- Magnetic declination correction (historical bearings)

### Horizontal curves (5F)
- R = 5729.58/D (arc-definition degree of curve, ft)
- T = R·tan(Δ/2); L = R·Δ(rad) = (Δ/360)·2πR; **L.C.** = 2R·sin(Δ/2)
- M (middle ordinate) = R(1−cos Δ/2); E (external) = R(sec(Δ/2)−1)
- Stationing: PC = PI − T; PT = PC + L

### Vertical curves (5G)
- Equal-tangent parabola: y = (g₂−g₁)/(2L)·x² ; rate of change r = (g₂−g₁)/L
- Elev on curve = elev_PVC + g₁x + (r/2)x²
- **High/low point**: x = −g₁/r (from PVC); turning-point elevation

### Geodesy / state plane (4B, 4C)
- **Grid ↔ ground**: ground = grid / combined factor; **combined factor = scale factor × elevation factor**
- Elevation factor = R/(R+h) (R≈20,906,000 ft); scale factor from SPCS projection
- Datums (NAD83, NAVD88), geoid vs ellipsoid (H = h − N), convergence (mapping angle)
- Lambert (E-W states) vs Transverse Mercator (N-S states) projections

### Photogrammetry / remote sensing (2F)
- Photo **scale** = f/(H−h) = (photo distance)/(ground distance)
- **GSD** (ground sample distance) = (sensor pixel size × H) / f
- Relief displacement d = r·h/H; flying height, overlap/sidelap, stereo base

### Volumes / earthwork (5H)
- Average end area: V = L·(A₁+A₂)/2 ; prismoidal correction
- Borrow/fill, shrinkage/swell factors

## 6. STUDY STRATEGY & RESOURCES

### Test-taking tips specific to the FS
- **Boundary Law (Area 3) is the largest area** — don't treat the FS as purely a math exam.
  Budget real study time on PLSS, metes & bounds, evidence/hierarchy of calls, easements,
  adverse possession, water law. These are conceptual, fast-to-answer points.
- **Master the supplied handbook BEFORE exam day.** It's searchable but big; know *where*
  each formula lives so you don't burn time hunting. Practice with the actual PDF from MyNCEES.
- **Pace**: 110 questions in 5 h 20 min ≈ **~2.9 min/question**. Flag-and-skip long COGO
  problems; bank the quick boundary/ethics/definition questions first.
- **Drill your calculator** (DMS, polar↔rect, stats, matrices) until conversions are reflexive.
- Watch the **NCEES YouTube** demo of how standards/handbook are presented on screen.
- Know your **units** — exam mixes SI and USCS; watch ft vs m and survey-foot vs intl-foot.

### Recommended resources (standard / mostly free)
- **NCEES FS Reference Handbook** (free PDF in MyNCEES) — the single most important doc; study it directly.
- **NCEES FS Practice Exam / interactive practice exam** (paid, but the only official-format items) — NCEES now offers an interactive online FS practice exam.
- **Ghilani & Wolf, *Elementary Surveying: An Introduction to Geomatics*** (15th ed.) — the canonical textbook covering nearly all FS computation topics.
- **Ghilani, *Adjustment Computations*** — for least squares / error propagation depth.
- **PPI *FS Surveying Reference Manual*** and **PPI/Cole *Fundamentals of Surveying Practice Exam* (5th ed., 110-question)** — well-regarded third-party prep.
- **Courville, *Fundamentals of Surveying: Exam Study Manual*** (180 pp, organized to current spec).
- **School of PE** FS review course (paid, instructor-led) — optional structured option.
- Brown's *Boundary Control and Legal Principles* — deeper boundary-law reference.

### Suggested study sequence
1. Read the spec + download the handbook; map your weak areas to the 7 areas.
2. Calculator fluency + Area 5/7 computations (highest density of solvable points).
3. Boundary Law (Area 3 — largest, high ROI) and the supplied standards.
4. Areas 1, 2, 4 (instrumentation, mapping/GIS/photogrammetry, geodesy).
5. Area 6 (business/ethics) — light, mostly reading/common sense.
6. Timed full-length practice exam(s) under exam conditions; review diagnostics.
